                                                            Customer Behavior Churn Risk Analysis
Excel, PostgreSQL and Power BI analysis of e-commerce customer data to understand customer behavior, purchasing patterns, engagement levels, and churn risk.

                                                                      Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior in an e-commerce business to understand why customers engage with the platform but fail to convert into buyers, and why some customers eventually stop purchasing.

Using Excel for data cleaning, PostgreSQL for business analysis, and Power BI for visualization, I explored customer purchasing patterns, engagement metrics, discount behavior, customer value segmentation, and churn indicators to generate meaningful business insights.

The goal of this project is:
i. To understand customer behavior
ii. To identify high-value and at-risk customers
iii. To discover factors affecting purchases and churn
iv. To suggest practical business recommendations to improve revenue and customer retention

                                                        Key Findings

Customer Engagement vs Purchases:
Some customers frequently visit and engage with the platform but make very low purchases, showing a conversion problem rather than a traffic problem.

High-Value Customers:
A small group of customers contributes significantly to overall revenue through higher lifetime value and frequent purchases.

Customer Churn Risk:
Customers with high inactivity periods, low engagement, and low purchase frequency are more likely to churn.

Cart Abandonment:
Higher cart abandonment rates are associated with lower purchasing behavior, indicating friction during the buying process.

Discount Behavior:
Discounts influence customer purchasing behavior, especially among mid-value customers, but excessive discounts may affect long-term profitability.

Customer Segmentation
High-value customers generally show:
Higher login frequency
Higher product interaction
More reviews written
Lower cart abandonment compared to low-value customers

                                           Business Recommendations:

Improve Customer Conversion

The business should improve product visibility and simplify the customer purchase journey to reduce drop-off during checkout.

Focus on High-Value Customers

High-value customers contribute most of the revenue, so the company should improve retention strategies through loyalty rewards and personalized experiences.

Reduce Cart Abandonment

The company should reduce checkout friction, display costs earlier, and improve payment flexibility.

Re-engage At-Risk Customers

Inactive customers should be targeted through personalized campaigns, incentives, and customer engagement strategies.

Use Discounts Strategically

Discounts should be used strategically during campaigns or seasonal periods instead of constant discounting to protect profitability.
